IELTS TEST FORMAT

READING

The Reading test comprises 40 questions, each worth 1 mark. The maximum score is 40, which determines your band score ranging from 1 to 9. Time taken: 60 minutes

WRITING

The examiners assess the Writing section using detailed criteria of IELTS 9 bands across four areas: Task Achievement, Task Response, Coherence & Cohesion, and Lexical Resource & Grammatical Range & Accuracy.

Each area carries equal weightage. The Writing component includes Academic and General modules, with Task Response holding more significance. Make sure to write clearly as illegible writing may affect your marking. Time allotted: 60 minutes

SPEAKING

Examiners assign band scores for four criteria: Fluency & Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical range & Accuracy and Pronunciation. Each criterion carries equal weightage. The test consists of three parts and revolves around a friendly conversation, covering topics related to your past, present and future.
Duration:11 to 14 minutes

LISTENING

In the listening section, you'll face 40 questions. The materials are recorded, played only once, and you get 10 minutes to jot down your answers

Time Duration: 30 minutes
